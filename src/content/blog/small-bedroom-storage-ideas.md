The bedroom is usually the most storage-starved room in a small Canadian apartment. Here's how to get every last centimetre working for you — with IKEA, Amazon.ca, and HomeSense options at real CAD prices, all renter-friendly.

**TL;DR:** Slim velvet hangers (Amazon.ca, ~$18 CAD for 50) add 30–40% more closet space immediately — it's the single highest-value purchase per dollar in this entire guide. Under-bed IKEA SKUBB bags (~$14 CAD) tap the most ignored storage space in most bedrooms. A second tension rod below the closet rod ($15 CAD) doubles hanging space for shirts and short items. A storage bed frame (IKEA BRIMNES, ~$399 CAD) can replace a dresser entirely.

---

## 1. Switch to an Under-Bed Storage Bed Frame

If you're buying a new bed frame, get one with storage drawers built in. For your existing bed, check if there's at least 20 cm of clearance underneath for flat storage boxes.

**Best budget option:** IKEA BRIMNES bed frame with drawers (~$399 CAD).

## 2. Under-Bed Bins for Seasonal Storage

Flat, lidded bins designed for under-bed storage keep off-season clothes, extra bedding, and shoes organized and dust-free. Label each bin by category.

**Best option:** IKEA SKUBB under-bed bag ($14 for 2).

![A bedroom with a reading nook shelf beside the bed — every vertical surface used for storage and calm display](/images/bedroom-shelves-reading.jpg)

## 3. Vertical Dresser Over a Wide One

A 6-drawer tall dresser stores the same number of items as a wide 3-drawer but takes half the floor footprint. In a small bedroom, every square metre of floor space matters.

## 4. Slim Velvet Hangers Throughout the Closet

Standard plastic hangers are enormously wasteful of closet space. Switching your entire wardrobe to slim velvet hangers typically adds 30–40% more hanging capacity — for ~$18 CAD.

![An open wardrobe with clothes organized by category — slim hangers and shelf dividers make every inch usable](/images/closet-wardrobe-open.jpg)

## 5. Double Hanging Rod With a Tension Bar

A tension closet rod hung below your existing rod doubles the hanging space for shorter items — shirts, blazers, folded trousers. No tools, fully adjustable, easy to remove.

**Cost:** Amazon.ca tension rod, ~$15–$20 CAD.

## 6. Shelf Dividers for Folded Stacks

Folded clothing stacks always collapse eventually. Shelf dividers (metal or acrylic) keep each stack in its lane. Apply them to closet shelves to divide sweaters, jeans, and shirts cleanly.

**Cost:** Set of 6 from Amazon.ca, ~$18–$25 CAD.

## 7. Over-Door Organizers on the Closet Door

Every closet door is wasted storage. Over-door organizers work for:
- Shoes (deep pocket versions)
- Accessories like scarves, belts, and ties
- Folded items like workout gear

**Cost:** Amazon.ca over-door organizer, ~$25–$35 CAD.

## 8. Hooks on the Bedroom Door

The back of your bedroom door is the most underused surface in the room. A hook strip or a single over-door hook holds robes, tomorrow's outfit, or bags.

## 9. Floating Bedside Shelves

Floor space beside the bed is precious. Wall-mounted bedside shelves (Command strips for lightweight, one screw for heavier) keep a lamp, phone, water, and book within reach without floor furniture.

**Cost:** From $20 CAD on Wayfair Canada or Amazon.ca.

![Floating shelves as bedroom storage — books, plants, and a lamp instead of a full nightstand footprint](/images/decor-floating-shelves.jpg)

## 10. A Pegboard or Grid Panel Behind the Desk

If you have a desk in the bedroom, a IKEA SKÅDIS pegboard or a grid wall panel above it keeps supplies, headphones, and chargers organized vertically rather than spread across the desk.

## 11. Narrow Rolling Storage Between the Dresser and Wall

A slim rolling cart (20–25 cm wide) fits between furniture pieces and the wall in many bedrooms. Use it for folded clothes, accessories, or personal items.

**Cost:** Slim rolling cart from Amazon.ca, ~$30–$45 CAD.

## 12. Vacuum Storage Bags for Bulky Items

Duvets, winter coats, and chunky sweaters take enormous space. Vacuum storage bags compress them to a fraction of their size. Store the compressed bags under the bed or on a high closet shelf.

**Cost:** Amazon.ca vacuum bags (6-pack), ~$25 CAD.

## 13. Overhead Closet Shelf Bins

The top shelf of a closet is often underused because items get buried. Use clearly labelled bins for items you don't need regularly — seasonal accessories, formal wear, extra bedding. A step stool makes them accessible.

## 14. A Hanging Wardrobe If You Have No Closet

If your bedroom has no closet at all, a fabric hanging wardrobe cover on a rolling rack provides full wardrobe storage. Add a shoe shelf underneath for a complete system.

**Cost:** Fabric wardrobe cover (~$35–$50 CAD) + a rolling garment rack (~$30–$45 CAD).

## The Small Bedroom Storage Priority Order

If you're starting from scratch, tackle in this order:

1. **Under-bed storage** — biggest impact, zero floor space used
2. **Slim hangers** — free up closet space immediately
3. **Over-door organizer** — add storage with no real estate
4. **Floating nightstand** — eliminate bedside table footprint
5. **Vertical dresser** — store more in less floor space

These 14 ideas cover every storage zone in a small bedroom — from the closet to the walls to the floor. Work through the priority order first, then layer in the bigger furniture changes as budget allows.
