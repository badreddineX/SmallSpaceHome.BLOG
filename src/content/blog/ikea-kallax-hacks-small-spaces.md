If there's one piece of IKEA furniture built for small apartment life, it's the KALLAX. I own two. Here's how to get the most out of them.

## Why the KALLAX Works for Small Spaces

The KALLAX is modular, affordable, and endlessly configurable. You can:

- Stand it horizontal as a sideboard or TV console
- Stand it vertical as a bookshelf
- Use it as a room divider between a living area and bedroom
- Add it to a wardrobe system with doors and legs
- Mount it to the wall (with proper wall anchors)

Each 33×33 cm cube fits the full range of IKEA bins, doors, drawers, and baskets. And the price is hard to beat anywhere in Canada.

## 1. Entryway Storage Bench

Lay a 1×4 KALLAX on its side, add legs ($20 CAD, IKEA CAPITA), put a cushion on top, and add fabric bins on the inside facing out. You've got an entryway bench with shoe storage underneath.

**Total cost:** ~$130 CAD with cushion and bins.

## 2. Room Divider Between Living Room and Bedroom

A 2×4 KALLAX standing vertically separates a studio apartment into two zones without adding a wall. Style one side for the living room, the other for the bedroom.

**Tip:** Secure it to the wall or ceiling with anti-tip hardware for safety.

## 3. TV Console With Hidden Storage

A 1×4 KALLAX at the right height works as a TV console. Add doors on four cubes for hidden storage (router, cables, game controllers) and leave the others open for a decorative touch.

**Cost:** 4-square KALLAX + 4 doors: ~$139 CAD.

## 4. Home Office Storage Wall

Stack two 2×4 units side by side to create a full storage wall behind a desk. Dedicate cubes to: paper, supplies, books, archived files, and personal items. Add a shelf across the top for a monitor riser.

## 5. Kids' or Guest Room Wardrobe

Add KALLAX doors ($15 each) and a hanging rail insert (~$30) and you have a wardrobe for a guest room or small bedroom. Far cheaper than a PAX wardrobe for temporary or smaller needs.

## 6. Bathroom Linen Storage

A 1×2 vertical KALLAX fits in most bathroom corners and holds towels, toilet paper, and toiletry bins perfectly. Use basket bins for a spa-like look.

## 7. Kitchen Pantry Overflow

If your kitchen cupboards aren't cutting it, a KALLAX in the kitchen or nearby hallway with deep bins handles pantry overflow beautifully. Label the bins by category.

## 8. Under-Window Storage and Window Seat

A 2×2 KALLAX under a window (horizontal) with a cushion becomes a window seat with storage underneath — four full cubes for blankets, books, or whatever you need hidden.

## 9. Bookshelf + Display Wall

Two 1×4 vertical KALLAXes side by side creates a dramatic book and display wall. Alternate open and closed cubes for visual rhythm without looking cluttered.

## 10. Craft or Hobby Station

A 2×4 KALLAX with labelled bins for craft supplies, fabric, tools, and paper creates a dedicated craft space that's easy to close up and ignore when guests arrive.

## 11. Hallway Storage

A slim 1×4 horizontal KALLAX in a hallway (about 42 cm deep) stores shoes, bags, scarves, and hats without blocking the path. Add a mirror on the wall above it and it becomes a proper entryway.
