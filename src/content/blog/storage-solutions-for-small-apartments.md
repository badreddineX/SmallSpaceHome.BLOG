Storage in a small Canadian apartment is not a products problem — it is a sequence problem. Most renters buy bins before they've decided what they're storing. Here's the sequence that works: decide what to keep (declutter), identify where it needs to live, then find the right solution for that location. [CMHC research](https://www.cmhc-schl.gc.ca) confirms that Canadian renters cite storage as the top pain point in apartment living — and most of the fixes cost under $50 CAD. Here are 16 solutions organized by room, all tested in real Canadian apartments.

**TL;DR:** Under-bed IKEA SKUBB bags (~$14 CAD) are the highest-value first purchase. Slim velvet hangers ($18 CAD) free 30–40% more closet space immediately. Over-door organizers ($28–$35 CAD, Amazon.ca) work on every door without installation. The IKEA KALLAX (~$79–$159 CAD) solves living room storage, media console, and room divider all in one piece.

## Bedroom Storage Solutions

### 1. Under-Bed Storage With Flat Bins or SKUBB Bags

The area under the bed is the most underused storage space in most small apartments. [IKEA SKUBB](https://www.ikea.com/ca/en/) under-bed bags ($14 CAD for 2) are flat, zip closed, and store off-season clothing and extra bedding with zero visual footprint.

If your bed sits too low, bed risers add 15 cm of clearance for ~$25 CAD at [Amazon.ca](https://www.amazon.ca).

### 2. A Storage Bed Frame

IKEA NORDLI bed frame with drawers (~$399–$699 CAD, Queen) has two large drawers on each side. This replaces the need for a dresser in most small bedrooms, and the drawers are more accessible than under-bed bins.

### 3. Over-Door Closet Organizer

The back of a bedroom closet door is storage you're ignoring. A 24-pocket over-door organizer ($28–$35 CAD, Amazon.ca) holds shoes, accessories, scarves, and belts without taking a square centimetre of closet floor space.

### 4. Tension Rod for Double Hanging

A second tension rod below the main closet rod creates a full extra tier of hanging space for shirts and short items. $15 CAD at [Amazon.ca](https://www.amazon.ca), requires no installation.

### 5. Slim Velvet Hangers

Replace all plastic hangers with slim velvet ones. Frees 30–40% more hanging space in any closet. 50-pack for $18 CAD at Amazon.ca — the highest-value small-apartment purchase per dollar in this entire guide.

## Kitchen Storage Solutions

### 6. Pegboard for Wall Storage

A pegboard mounted on the kitchen wall (or leaned against it and attached with Command strips) stores pots, pans, utensils, and even spice jars without consuming any counter space.

IKEA SKADIS pegboard: ~$15 CAD for the basic panel. Add hooks and containers separately.

![A kitchen pegboard storing utensils and small items vertically — zero counter space used](/images/blog-11-img3.jpg)

### 7. Clear Stackable Pantry Bins

Loose pantry shelves hide things at the back and lead to duplicate purchases. Clear, stackable bins with labels make everything visible at a glance. Group by category: grains, canned goods, baking, snacks.

Amazon.ca clear bins: ~$5–$8 each. Buy 6–8 for a complete pantry system.

### 8. Cabinet Door Organizers

The inside of cabinet doors is wasted space. Wire racks or adhesive organizers on the inside of pantry doors store spice jars, foil, and wrap cleanly.

Amazon.ca cabinet door organizer set: ~$15–$22 CAD.

### 9. IKEA RASKOG Rolling Cart

A 3-tier rolling cart used as a kitchen island extension — for food prep, extra counter space during cooking, and rolling away when done. Can also hold cleaning supplies under the sink or extra linens in the linen cupboard.

IKEA RASKOG: ~$39 CAD.

## Living Room Storage Solutions

### 10. IKEA KALLAX as Media Console + Storage

KALLAX with fabric drawer inserts keeps cables and electronics organized while looking intentionally designed. The inserts hide the contents behind a clean fabric front.

KALLAX 2×2: ~$79 CAD. Fabric inserts: ~$6 each.

![IKEA KALLAX unit with fabric inserts acting as a media console and storage in a small living room](/images/blog-11-img4.jpg)

### 11. Storage Ottoman as Coffee Table

A storage ottoman replaces the coffee table and adds a full storage chest in the same footprint. Use it for extra blankets, books, games, or anything that needs daily-adjacent access.

Wayfair Canada: ~$89–$150 CAD.

### 12. Floating Shelves Above the Sofa

The wall above the sofa is typically empty. Two or three floating shelves add a display shelf for books, plants, and art while keeping storage off the floor.

Command Large Hanging Strips hold shelves up to 7.5 kg. IKEA LACK shelves 2-pack: ~$24 CAD.

## Bathroom Storage Solutions

### 13. Over-Toilet Shelf Unit

The space above the toilet is wasted in most rental bathrooms. A freestanding over-toilet shelf unit adds 2–3 tiers of storage for toiletries, extra towels, and bathroom supplies.

Amazon.ca or Canadian Tire: ~$40–$70 CAD.

### 14. Over-Door Bathroom Organizer

The back of the bathroom door holds hair tools, products, and anything that clutters the bathroom counter.

Same organizer as the bedroom closet version (~$28–$35 CAD, Amazon.ca).

## Entryway Storage Solutions

### 15. Slim 2-Tier Shoe Rack

In a Canadian winter, a shoe rack at the entry is non-negotiable. Wet boots need a place to dry, and shoes piled at the door create chaos fast.

Amazon.ca slim 2-tier shoe rack: ~$20–$35 CAD.

### 16. IKEA KALLAX as Entry Unit With Bench Attachment

A 4-cube KALLAX placed horizontally at the entry (with the optional bench top) creates a proper entryway: storage in each cube, seating on top, and hooks on the wall above.

KALLAX 1×4 horizontal: ~$79 CAD. Bench pad: ~$15–$30 CAD.
