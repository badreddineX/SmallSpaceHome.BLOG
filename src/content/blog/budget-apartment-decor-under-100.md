You don't need a renovation budget to make your Canadian apartment feel like a home. Some of the biggest visual improvements cost almost nothing — and every idea here is fully renter-friendly. Here are 12 ideas, all under $100 CAD total, most under $20 each.

**TL;DR:** Swap cool-white bulbs for warm 2700K LEDs ($15–$18 CAD) — this alone transforms how every room looks and feels. Print your own art at Costco Photo (~$3 per print), frame with IKEA RIBBA frames ($10 each), and get a gallery wall for under $40 CAD. A chunky throw from HomeSense (~$20–$35 CAD) changes the sofa instantly. Rearrange the furniture for free before spending anything.

---

## 1. Print Your Own Art ($0–$15 CAD)

The most expensive-looking walls in design magazines often have printed art, not original paintings. Sites like Unsplash and Pexels offer high-resolution photos free to use. Download, send to a print shop (Staples, Costco Photo), and frame.

A 20×30 cm print at Costco Photo costs about $3 CAD. Three in matching IKEA RIBBA frames ($10 each) gives you a gallery wall for ~$40 CAD.

## 2. A Large Throw Blanket on the Sofa ($20–$35 CAD)

A throw blanket draped over a sofa changes the entire feel of a living room. Go for texture — a chunky knit or waffle weave — in a neutral that complements your existing colours.

**Where to find them:** HomeSense is the best value. IKEA's POLARVIDE throw is $14 CAD and comes in multiple colours.

![A cozy living room with a chunky throw blanket draped over the sofa — one of the easiest budget upgrades](/images/living-room-cozy.jpg)

## 3. New Throw Cushion Covers ($15–$30 CAD)

If you already have cushion inserts, just replace the covers. A fresh set of cushion covers in a coordinated colour scheme can make a sofa look completely new.

**Standard sizes:** 45×45 cm (18 inch) is the most common. Amazon.ca has good variety from $12–$18 per cover.

## 4. A Candle Collection ($10–$20 CAD)

Three candles of different heights grouped on a tray make any surface look intentional. Pillar candles in white or cream are universally flattering. Dollar stores sell them for $2–$4 each.

Group them in threes on a small wooden tray (thrift stores always have these) for a complete look.

## 5. New Lightbulbs ($15–$20 CAD)

Swap every harsh cool-white bulb in your apartment for warm white LED bulbs (2700K colour temperature). The quality of light transforms how every room looks and feels — more than almost anything else.

A pack of 6 A19 warm white LED bulbs from Amazon.ca runs about $15–$18 CAD.

## 6. Rearrange the Furniture (Free)

The fastest and cheapest room transformation is moving the furniture. Try the sofa against a different wall. Float the bed in the center. Put the desk facing a window.

Most of us place furniture where it fits logically when we move in and never question it again. A different arrangement can make a room feel completely different.

## 7. A Tray to Define the Coffee Table ($15–$25 CAD)

A decorative tray on the coffee table corrals the remote, candles, and small objects into one intentional arrangement. Round trays work especially well.

Thrift stores often have great trays. HomeSense usually has good options for under $20 CAD.

## 8. Washi Tape Bookmarks or Accents (Free if You Have It)

Use washi tape to create a faux headboard behind the bed, frame a blank wall, or make a stripe at the baseboard. It comes off cleanly and can be repositioned.

## 9. A Vase With Dried Grasses ($10–$20 CAD)

A tall simple vase with dried pampas grass or eucalyptus is everywhere in interior design right now for good reason — it's low maintenance, long-lasting, and looks expensive.

Dried bunches from Amazon.ca run ~$12–$18 CAD. A thrift store vase costs $2–$5.

![Floating shelves styled with plants and books — a budget decor move that looks intentional and expensive](/images/decor-floating-shelves.jpg)

## 10. Curtain Clips for Fabric Panels ($8–$12 CAD)

Don't have curtain rods or can't install them? Clip-ring curtains hang from a tension rod (no drilling) and look intentional rather than improvised.

## 11. Stack of Books as Decor (Free if You Have Them)

A curated stack of books — spines facing out, same direction — on a coffee table or shelf is a classic interior design move. Pick books with covers you like and make it part of the display.

## 12. Plug-In Fairy Lights ($10–$18 CAD)

Warm white fairy lights draped over a bookshelf, behind a headboard, or around a mirror add ambient light and instant warmth. Plug-in versions require no installation.

## The $100 CAD Room Makeover Plan

Here's how I'd spend exactly $100 CAD for the biggest visual impact in one room:

| Item | CAD Cost |
|------|---------|
| 3 art prints (Costco Photo) | $9 |
| 3 IKEA RIBBA frames | $30 |
| IKEA POLARVIDE throw | $14 |
| 2 throw cushion covers (Amazon.ca) | $28 |
| Warm white LED bulbs (6-pack) | $17 |
| **Total** | **$98** |

Hang the art gallery-style, drape the throw, add the cushions, swap the bulbs. That's a room that looks like you spent a weekend and $800 on it. You didn't.
